Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurologic disorder that causes the brain to shrink and brain cells to die. Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia, which is nothing but a continuous decline in thinking, behavioral pattern and social skills that affects a person's ability to function independently.
